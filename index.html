C.C. Busy

C.C. Busy allows family child care providers verbally to log child activities throughout the day, including attendance, meals and diapering and reminds providers about critical child specific information. CC Busy logs these activities, texting the child’s daily log to parents at day’s end, and prompts parents when the child’s child care supplies are low. Parents can review the daily log on their smartphones or with their digital assistant at home. Parents can also review, update and sign standard required documentation.

C.C. Busy on YouTube

Key technologies: python, flask, oauth, postgreSQL, SQLalchemy, Celery, Linode, flask-ask, flask-assistant, Alexa, Google Assistant, Angularjs, Bootstrap

Next Best Everything

Next Best Everything offers users the power to curate ranked lists of things they are passionate about and anything else you can imagine!

The website uses Facebook login for security. Server-side PostgreSQL manages list storage and gameplay. Next Best Everything uses Celery to create realtime images to facilitate sharing of favorite items and list ranks.

To engage users and to support the site, background tasks find popular items on Amazon that match the user's categories and items.

Key technologies: python, flask, oauth, postgreSQL, SQLalchemy, Celery, Linode

Organic Idiocy: Programming Digital Assistants

Organic Idiocy is my YouTube Channel featuring duet performances of digital assistants Alexa and Google Assistant offering modern parodies of classic comedy routines. I adapt the parody, then write code for each device, using Flask-Ask and Flask-Assistant packages in Python to give each AI their marching orders. I serve up the coding via ngrok on my local Mac or via Heroku. Then I film on my iPhone the devices interacting and publish to YouTube.

Organic Idiocy on YouTube

Key technologies: python, flask, flask-ask, flask-assistant, Alexa, Google Assistant
